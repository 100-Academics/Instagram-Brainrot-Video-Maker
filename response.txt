STORY_BEGIN
I (28M) am the "bad guy" in my family's eyes because I refused to let my sister borrow my car for her destination wedding.
For context, I've been saving up for three years to buy a reliable used SUV. I work two jobs, one in logistics and one driving for a delivery app, to pay it off early. It's my first major purchase, and I treat it like gold. It has a pristine interior, a new set of tires, and I have a strict "no passengers" policy for the back seat because I'm terrified of spills and stains.
My sister, "Chloe" (26F), has always been a bit entitled. She's the "golden child" who never had to work a day in her life, thanks to our parents' generosity. She's getting married next month in a remote location in the Pacific Northwest. The venue is about 4 hours away from where I live, and the nearest rental car agency is an hour from the venue.
Three weeks ago, Chloe asked me to drive her and her fiancé up there for the wedding weekend. She claimed her fiancé's car was "too small" and that she needed someone she trusted to drive her up so she could relax and "get into the bridal mood." I immediately said no. I told her I was saving my gas money for a specific trip I'm planning with my own partner later this year, and that I just don't want to risk any accidents or wear and tear on a long, winding mountain drive.
She threw a massive fit. She called me selfish, said I was "ruining her special day," and threatened to tell Mom and Dad that I'm a "jerk." Our parents, of course, took her side. They called me, saying I need to "step up" and "be a good brother," and that a 4-hour drive is nothing. They even offered to pay for my gas, which I refused because the wear and tear isn't about money.
Yesterday, Chloe showed up at my apartment unannounced with her fiancé and a suitcase. She demanded I take them. When I again refused, she started screaming in the hallway. She tried to grab my keys. I physically blocked her and told her to leave. She called the police, claiming I was being abusive and threatening her. The cops came, heard both sides, and since there was no actual violence or threat, they told us to sort it out civilly. Chloe left in a huff, telling everyone at the family group chat that I'm a monster who doesn't care about her happiness.
Now, my parents are giving me the silent treatment, and Chloe is posting passive-aggressive stories on Instagram about "family betrayal." I feel like I'm the only one being reasonable, but everyone thinks I'm the villain for protecting my property. Am I the asshole?
